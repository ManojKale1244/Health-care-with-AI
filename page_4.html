Meditation Management

Curative care includes the use of prescribed medications to treat diseases and manage symptoms effectively.
Meditation Reconciliation

1. Reviewing and comparing a patients medication list to ensure accuracy and avoid duplications, omissions, or interactions. Prescription Management
2. Ensuring the correct medications are prescribed, based on the patients medical history and condition. Medication Adherence
3. Monitoring whether patients take their medications as prescribed, providing reminders, and educating them on the importance of adherence. Medication Monitoring
4. Regularly checking for effectiveness, side effects, and interactions through follow-ups and assessments. Patient Education
5. Informing patients about the purpose, proper use, potential side effects, and any necessary precautions for their medications. Pharmacovigilance
6. Monitoring and reporting any adverse effects or drug interactions to improve medication safety. Use of Technology
7. Employing tools like medication management apps, electronic health records (EHRs), and automated reminders for better medication adherence.

Surgical Procedures

interventions are often necessary to treat certain medical conditions and ensure the recovery of patients. Surgery is an essential medical procedure used to diagnose, treat, or manage various health conditions. Its importance can be highlighted in several ways:

1. Life-Saving: Surgeries like emergency operations for trauma, organ transplants, or removing tumors can save lives.
2. Disease Management: Many diseases, including cancer, heart disease, and gastrointestinal issues, often require surgical intervention for effective treatment.
3. Improved Quality of Life: Corrective surgeries for orthopedic issues, joint replacements, and reconstructive procedures can significantly improve a person's mobility and comfort.
4. Preventive Care: Some surgeries are performed to prevent the progression of diseases, such as removing precancerous growths or repairing damaged blood vessels.
5. Diagnosis: Exploratory surgeries and biopsies help in diagnosing illnesses when non-invasive methods are inconclusive.
6. Restorative and Cosmetic: Plastic and reconstructive surgeries can restore function and appearance after injury, illness, or congenital conditions.

Specialist Consultations

Specialist doctors provide targeted treatments and advanced care to manage complex health issues.

1. Cardiology – Heart-related conditions (e.g., hypertension, heart disease).
2.Neurology – Brain, spinal cord, and nervous system disorders (e.g., stroke, epilepsy).
3.Orthopedics – Bone, joint, and muscle issues (e.g., fractures, arthritis).
4.Dermatology – Skin, hair, and nail disorders (e.g., acne, eczema, skin cancer).
5.Gastroenterology – Digestive system disorders (e.g., ulcers, IBS, liver diseases).
6.Endocrinology – Hormonal and metabolic disorders (e.g., diabetes, thyroid disorders).
7.Pulmonology – Lung and respiratory conditions (e.g., asthma, COPD).
8.Oncology – Diagnosis and treatment of cancer.
9.Ophthalmology – Eye diseases and vision issues.
10.Psychiatry – Mental health conditions (e.g., depression, anxiety, schizophrenia).
11.Nephrology – Kidney-related disorders.
